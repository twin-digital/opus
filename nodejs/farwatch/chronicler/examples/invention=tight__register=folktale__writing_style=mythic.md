When the company came at last before the warden of the Ashford passage — a man known for his long memory and his poor welcome of outsiders — Tessaly Vorn stepped forward and spoke with measured plainness, setting out the compact's purpose without flattery or demand, and the warden found in her neither threat nor guile, and so the gate was opened and the inner roads lay before them.
