They entered the Thornmere vault in darkness and without sound, threading the outer wards unseen, and so came unannounced to the passages that lay within. There they found guardians of iron and old making barring the corridor beyond the second arch, but fell upon them without hesitation and left them still on the flagstones, and pressed on to the sanctum threshold with their wounds still wet. But the sanctum door bore marks in no tongue any among the company could name, and whatever learning they had carried into that place shed no light on what was written there or what it bound; they could not read the inscription, and so could not know whether what lay sealed beyond was the thing they had come so far to find. They put their full strength to the door then, all of them together, until the effort had wrung them hollow — and the door gave nothing, and at last they turned back through the long dark of the passages, carrying neither prize nor word of what the vault still kept from the world.
