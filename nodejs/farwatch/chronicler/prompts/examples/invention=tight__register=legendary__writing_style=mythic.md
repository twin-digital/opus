When the company of the covenant came to the hall of Mereth Sorn, they came neither in arms nor in anger but with measured words and the gravity of those who know the weight of what they ask; the lord of that place had turned away harder suitors, yet he listened, and before the first hour had turned he gave his assent — therefore the road that had been sealed now lay open before them. Gold then did the work that speech alone could not have finished, for between that hall and the deep country beyond lay brokers and toll-keepers with no loyalty to persuade and no cause to honor, only prices, and the company paid without complaint, purchasing passage yard by yard through ground that coin could cross where argument could not. What awaited them in that deep country was neither locked door nor waiting palm but simple suffering — cold and duration and the body's long complaint — and they put their heads down and went through it, meeting the land's resistance with a patience that did not falter. They returned to the covenant at last bearing what they had gone to find, and the chronicle sets them down among those who did not stop when the world offered them reason.
