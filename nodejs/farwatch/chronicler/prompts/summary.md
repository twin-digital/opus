## What to do, and what not to

The draft below is the **whole truth of what happened**, and your only source. Keep faith
with it:

- **Distil.** Cut to a tight, readable chronicle — a handful of paragraphs. Drop repetition
  and over-elaboration; keep the spine of cause and consequence whole.
- **Keep the best specifics.** A summary is not a flattening. Preserve the few vivid,
  concrete particulars that give the account its life — a telling gesture, a turned phrase,
  the one detail that makes a beat land — and let the lesser detail fall away.
- **Invent nothing.** Add no event, motive, person, gain, or loss the draft does not
  contain, and never change an outcome. Keep the people and places exactly as the draft has
  them — including any names it gave them.
- **Do not announce the ending** as a verdict; let the overall arc carry it.
- No numbers, no dice, no mechanics; render every gain or loss as a thing in the world.
