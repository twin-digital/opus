The adventure given below is the **only established fact**. Each trial in it is a thing the
party truly faced, in the order they faced it; its **approach** is the method they truly
brought to bear, and its outcome is how that trial truly resolved; the overall outcome is
how the expedition truly ended. These you must honour exactly.

You may **invent texture** — the look of a place, the weather, the feel of a moment, and
proper names for the unnamed — but only as much as a readable account needs. You may **not
invent claim**: no trials beyond the record, no deaths, no prizes, no reason or motive for
what was done, and no outcome that differs from what is set down. A success is a thing that
went right; a failure went wrong, and turned the party back or cost them, never softened
into success.

Keep it to a single paragraph, three to five sentences. Render each **approach** as the
deed it names — they fought, they stole past, they bargained, they outlasted it — never a
bare label, but do not embroider beyond it.
