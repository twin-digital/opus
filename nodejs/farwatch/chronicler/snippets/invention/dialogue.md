The adventure given below is the **established record**. Each trial in it is a thing the
party truly faced, in the order they faced it; its **approach** is the method they truly
brought to bear, and its outcome is how that trial truly resolved; the overall outcome is
how the expedition truly ended. These you must honour exactly — never change an outcome,
reorder the trials, or add or drop one.

Within that frame, **invent freely** to make a living history:

- **Texture** — the look of a place, the weather, the hour, the feel of a moment. Name
  places and people by what they are and where they stand — the drowned vault beneath the
  causeway, the marsh-wardens — not by minting proper nouns. Use a proper name only where
  the record supplies one.
- **Motive** — _why_ the party did what they did: why they chose the approach they took,
  why they pressed on after a reversal, why they turned for home. Give them reasons, and
  set them down as the chronicler's settled understanding — not as guesses, not hedged with
  "perhaps".
- **The specifics of the deed** — _how_ each approach was actually applied. Not merely that
  they deceived, but what the deception was; not merely that they fought, but the shape of
  the fight. Make each approach a particular act, never a general one.
- **Voices** — **let the party speak.** Put banter and dialogue between them on the page: a
  terse exchange before a risk, a wry aside in the aftermath, a muttered doubt, an argument
  over which way to go or who should go first. Lean on each seeker's `temperament` for how
  they talk and on the trial's `lead` for whose voice carries the beat — the glib one needles,
  the dour one grumbles, the eager one talks the rest into it, the quiet one says the one thing
  that lands. Spoken lines, even a single traded remark, turn the cast from figures into people;
  use them often, and let the seekers sound like themselves.

Hold two things back for now: do not invent **deaths**, nor **prizes** (named gains carried
home). And never assert an **outcome** other than the one recorded — a failure went wrong,
and turned the party back or cost them, never softened into success.

Render each **approach** as a concrete, particular deed — the actual trick, the actual feat
of strength, the actual rite — never a bare label. Write at whatever length the tale needs.
