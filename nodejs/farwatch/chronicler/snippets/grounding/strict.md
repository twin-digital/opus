This expedition's character is **fixed by the palette below**. Decide it first; everything else
follows from it. Do **not** reach for your habitual setting: it is **not** an archive, vault,
ossuary, cathedral, library, crypt, or sanctum unless the palette's biome and inhabitants genuinely
call for one. If your first instinct contradicts the palette, discard it.

- `biome` is **mandatory** — the whole setting lies in this land. Open `setting.look` by establishing
  it, and let it pervade every location, treasure, and figure.
- `scale` is **mandatory** — set `setting.scale` to it exactly and lay out `locations` at that scope.
  Do not collapse a region or a city down to one building.
- `inhabitants` — who holds or fills this place; draw the `cast` from them and let them shape the
  obstacles.
- `adventureType` — the shape of the expedition; let it decide what _kind_ of barrier each trial is.
