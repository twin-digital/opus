feat(farwatch): give each trial an `approach` — the method the party used to (try to) overcome it.

`Trial` gains an `approach` drawn at random from a 22-method pool (`APPROACHES`: combat, stealth, deception, endurance, magic, …), a mechanical skeleton with no narrative texture. It joins the chronicle-legal view alongside the outcome, and the prompt's schema and example tell the chronicler to render each approach as a deed (a `combat` trial met with force, a failed `deception` a ruse seen through) — never as a bare label. This gives the chronicler a concrete, varied hook so adventures stop collapsing into generic "cross water / climb mountain / open door" beats.
