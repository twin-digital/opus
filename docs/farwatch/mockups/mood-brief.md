# Farwatch — which mood fits? (a vibe check)

**What I need from you:** Farwatch is a game I'm building. I've drafted the look in **three different
moods**, and I can't decide which one *feels* right. You don't need to know anything about how it's built —
I just want your gut on which vibe matches the game. Read the short bit below, read the one short story
([`target-saga.md`](../target-saga.md)), click through the three mockups, and tell me which one feels like
*this game*.

## The game, in a feeling

You are a kind of distant, fading patron — a presence more than a person — watching over a small, far-off
outpost and the handful of people who live and work there. You can't go there. You can't reach them
directly. You sit at a desk, read the letters that come back, and send orders out into the silence — and
then time passes, and you find out what became of them.

It's slow, quiet, and a little sad. People you've come to know go out on your orders and don't always come
back, and you usually learn how it went from a letter that arrives too late to do anything about it. It's a
game about **reading, distance, and living with decisions made from far away** — the opposite of a busy,
efficient app. Think candlelit desk, travel-worn paper, ink, and a lot of careful reading; not dashboards
and buttons.

The short story, [`target-saga.md`](../target-saga.md), is the fastest way to actually *feel* it — **read
that first.** It's one expedition that goes wrong, and the patron living with it.

## The three moods

Each mockup shows the same couple of screens dressed in a different vibe:

- **Warm / candlelit** — illuminated-manuscript: parchment, gold, oxblood; intimate and romantic, read
  by the fire.
- **Austere / monastic** — cold stone, severe, hushed; a scriptorium more than a hearth.
- **Starker** — high-contrast and reductive; the most modern and dramatic of the three.

## How to weigh in

1. Read [`target-saga.md`](../target-saga.md) to get the feeling.
2. Open the mockups (the **index** page links all three) and **click around** — open a letter, lay a few
   people side by side. The little bits of writing and the way things move are part of the mood, so don't
   just glance at a still screenshot.
3. Tell me, gut-level: **which one feels like the game I just described** — and, if you can say, *why*.

**What to ignore:** rough edges, placeholder text, anything plain or unfinished — the paper is meant to
look more worn and textured later, and I haven't polished the writing. I'm only after the **overall
feeling**, not whether anything is finished or in the right place. (A couple of the moods have small type
variations to compare — pick the *vibe* first; the fine print second.)
