“You have been away some time, my lord. Word came back from the barrow.” — the steward

Farwatch · at the turn of the season

What you sent them to do Open the barrow no frost would touch, and bring back what slept inside — to the keeping of the lamp.

They came to the barrow-door at dusk, where the stone stayed dry though the fields about it lay under early white. Garrick set his shoulder to it and swore it would give before he did — and give it did, though it cost them an hour and the skin of his hands.

Within, the air was old. Mirela went first with the lamp, reading the floor for the gaps that swallow a careless foot, and found three of them before they found her. Odric said the whole vault would come down on their heads; it did not, but he was not wrong to fear it.

What it cost, and who came home, waits on the turning of the page.

They brought back the sleeper’s circlet, and the strongbox came home heavier for it — though the lien still falls due at first snow, and it will not cover that.

Mirela came home. Odric did not — the vault kept him, as he had always feared it might.

The prose is the index — a named phrase (“to the keeping of the lamp”, “early white”, “came home heavier”) raises that one fact as hand-laid paper that you let lie. For facts the text doesn’t name, the steward’s signature is the single door to the standing stack. No corner glyphs, no parked strip.
